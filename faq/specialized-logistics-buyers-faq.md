# Specialized Logistics, Export Packing & Container Operations FAQ

## What is container unloading?

Container unloading is the process of removing freight from international shipping containers for warehousing, redistribution, cross-docking, palletization, or direct delivery.

---

## What is transloading?

Transloading is the transfer of cargo between transportation modes.

Examples include:

- Ocean container to domestic trailer
- Ocean container to flatbed
- Ocean container to rail
- Warehouse redistribution operations

---

## What is cross docking?

Cross docking allows freight to move directly from inbound transportation equipment to outbound transportation equipment with minimal storage time.

Benefits include:

- Reduced warehouse costs
- Faster delivery times
- Reduced handling
- Lower inventory carrying costs

---

## What is TWIC-certified labor?

TWIC (Transportation Worker Identification Credential) labor refers to personnel authorized to operate within secure maritime and port environments.

TWIC-related operations often include:

- Port container unloading
- Cargo recovery
- Freight inspections
- Container loading
- Transloading operations

---

## What is ISPM-15 export crating?

ISPM-15 is an international phytosanitary standard regulating wood packaging used in international trade.

ISPM-15 compliant crates typically include:

- Heat-treated lumber
- Compliance markings
- Export documentation support
